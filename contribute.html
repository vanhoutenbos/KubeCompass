üìã Contribution Guidelines

Quality Standards

  • Be specific: Share concrete details, not vague opinions
  • Be honest: Include both pros and cons
  • Be contextual: Explain your use case, scale, and requirements
  • Be current: Note which versions you tested
  • Be respectful: Critique tools, not people or companies

What Makes a Good Tool Review

  • Real production usage: Hands-on experience, not just "hello world"
  • Specific scenarios: "We used X for Y with Z constraints"
  • Honest trade-offs: What worked well AND what didn't
  • Alternatives considered: Why you chose this tool over others
  • Context: Team size, scale, industry, compliance requirements
  • Lessons learned: What would you do differently?

What We Don't Accept

  • ‚ùå Vendor marketing copy or press releases
  • ‚ùå Content paid for or incentivized by vendors
  • ‚ùå Reviews without real usage experience
  • ‚ùå Personal attacks or unprofessional criticism
  • ‚ùå Unsubstantiated claims or rumors
  • ‚ùå Duplicate or low-effort content

Review Process

All contributions go through a review process to maintain quality:

  • Step 1: Submit via GitHub issue or pull request
  • Step 2: Community and maintainers review
  • Step 3: Address feedback and questions
  • Step 4: Merge and publish (typically within 1 week)

ü§ù Code of Conduct

KubeCompass is a community for professionals. We expect all contributors to:

  • Be respectful: Treat others with respect, even when disagreeing
  • Be constructive: Focus on improving the project, not tearing it down
  • Be inclusive: Welcome contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels
  • Be professional: Keep discussions focused on technical merit
  • Be transparent: Disclose any conflicts of interest or vendor relationships

Violations of our code of conduct may result in removal from the community. We want this to be a safe, productive space for everyone.

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to @vanhoutenbos.
